Breaking Corporate Silence

How High-Influence Leaders Create Cultures of Voice

Authors
Rob Bogosian & Christine Mockler Casper
Publisher
RVB Associates Press

Breaking Corporate Silence is the first extensively researched book that examines the causes and consequences of silence and voice in organizations. It provides a compelling framework for understanding why people stay silent at work — and what high-influence leaders can do about it.

Based on years of original research and real-world consulting experience, this book gives leaders the tools to diagnose their organizational culture, understand their own role in perpetuating silence, and take concrete steps to build a Culture of Voice.

Inside the Book

What You'll Discover

The Research Foundation

Drawing on extensive research with thousands of leaders and employees across industries, Breaking Corporate Silence presents a data-driven analysis of organizational silence: how it starts, how it spreads, and what it costs organizations in performance, innovation, and human potential.

The authors developed the Silence & Voice Inventory (SVI) — a validated assessment instrument used to measure and diagnose organizational culture along the silence-voice spectrum — the backbone of every engagement RVB Associates conducts.

The result is not just theory but a practitioner's guide for leaders who want to make real, lasting change in how their organizations communicate and perform.
